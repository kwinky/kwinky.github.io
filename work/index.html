Kirsten Frederiksen is a data protection lawyer at the
BBC
in
London, United Kingdom
where her particular focus is advising new media and technology projects on UK and EU data protection laws. She is an Australian qualified lawyer and worked in private practice as a technology and privacy lawyer at K&L Gates in Australia for a number of years, advising clients on large-scale outsourcing projects and IT transactions, as well as privacy advisory work. Prior to that, Kirsten advised on privacy and freedom of information law and policy at a government department.

Kirsten has a long-held interest in digital media: she has a bachelor's degree in computer science and previously worked in software development and graphic design roles.
